Why Cheap Epoxy Fails in Silverthorne

  • Heavy I-70 road salt — Silverthorne sits right along the interstate corridor, and that salt gets tracked into garages on tires and boots all winter, eating through thin topcoats.
  • Freeze-thaw movement — Concrete here expands and contracts significantly through repeated winter cycles. Coatings without proper mechanical prep delaminate from beneath.
  • River-valley moisture — Proximity to the Blue River means more ground moisture than towns set higher and farther from water. A proper vapor-blocking base coat is essential.
  • High-altitude UV — Budget epoxy topcoats yellow and chalk in Silverthorne's mountain sun. Our polyaspartic topcoats are UV-stable and rated for it.

Every system we install in Silverthorne uses commercial-grade materials chosen for this exact combination of conditions — not an off-the-shelf kit.

Caring for Your Floor Through a Silverthorne Winter

A properly installed floor doesn't need much upkeep, but a few habits make a real difference in how it looks after several Silverthorne winters:

  • Rinse off standing salt brine — A quick hose-down or wet-mop after a heavy salt week keeps I-70 de-icer residue from sitting on the surface for weeks at a time. It's cosmetic, not structural, but it keeps the floor looking new.
  • Use a squeegee for big snowmelt days — On days when several inches of snow melt off a vehicle at once, pushing the water toward the floor drain or garage door threshold prevents it from pooling in one spot for hours.
  • Don't worry about ice-melt products — Unlike bare concrete, a fully cured polyaspartic topcoat isn't damaged by common calcium chloride or magnesium chloride ice-melt products tracked in on boots.
  • Check driveway runoff at the garage threshold — Wildernest and Mesa Cortina properties on steeper grades sometimes see extra runoff at the garage entrance during spring melt. A simple threshold seal or door sweep keeps it outside where it belongs.

None of this is required to keep the lifetime warranty valid — it's simply what keeps a Silverthorne garage floor looking as good in year five as it did on installation day.
