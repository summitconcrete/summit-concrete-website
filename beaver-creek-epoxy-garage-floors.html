Why Cheap Epoxy Fails in Beaver Creek

  • Road salt and de-icers — Beaver Creek's roads and shared driveways are heavily treated all winter. That salt gets tracked in on tires and ski boots and eats through thin topcoats fast.
  • Freeze-thaw movement — Concrete above 8,000 feet expands and contracts more than at lower elevations. Coatings without proper mechanical prep delaminate from beneath.
  • High UV at altitude — Budget epoxy topcoats yellow and chalk in Beaver Creek's intense mountain sun — a visible flaw in a garage designed to look premium.
  • Heat and moisture cycling — Heated driveways and garages common in Beaver Creek estates create their own moisture and temperature dynamics that a proper vapor-blocking system is built to handle.

Every system we install in Beaver Creek uses commercial-grade materials selected for this climate and finish standard — not an off-the-shelf kit.

Caring for a Showroom-Finish Floor

A metallic or full-broadcast flake floor in a Beaver Creek garage holds up to daily use the same way any of our systems do — but a few habits keep it looking gallery-ready:

  • Rinse off standing salt brine — A quick hose-down or wet-mop after a heavy salt week keeps de-icer residue from dulling the finish, especially noticeable on a metallic pour.
  • Use walk-off mats near the entry — Trapping grit and salt at the door before it reaches the main floor area reduces the fine scuffing that shows up first on a high-gloss finish.
  • Avoid dragging heavy gear — Skis, snowmobiles, and equipment sleds should be lifted rather than dragged across a metallic finish to avoid surface scratching, even though the underlying polyaspartic topcoat resists it well.
  • Periodic topcoat refresh is optional, not required — Unlike a painted floor, our polyaspartic topcoat doesn't need recoating to maintain its warranty. A refresh coat is purely cosmetic if you ever want to renew the gloss after many years of heavy use.

None of this is required to keep the lifetime warranty valid — it's simply what keeps a Beaver Creek garage floor looking as considered as the rest of the home.
