Our 1–2 Day Epoxy Installation Process in Summit County

Most epoxy garage floor projects in Summit County are completed in one to two days. Here's exactly what our process looks like:

1

Diamond Grinding & Concrete Prep

We use commercial diamond grinders to mechanically profile the concrete and open the pores. This is the most critical step — especially in Summit County where freeze-thaw moisture can compromise adhesion. Acid etching (used in DIY kits) is not sufficient at altitude.

2

Moisture-Blocking Epoxy Base Coat

We apply a moisture-vapor-blocking epoxy primer and base coat — critical for Summit County slabs that sit over ground with high freeze-thaw moisture content. While still wet, we broadcast your chosen flake blend across the full surface.

3

Polyaspartic Topcoat

After scraping and vacuuming excess flakes, we apply a commercial polyaspartic or polyurethane topcoat. This clear coat is UV stable, chemical resistant, and cures reliably even at Summit County's 9,000 ft elevation where temperature and humidity make standard topcoats fail.

4

Drive-In Ready in 48–72 Hours

Light foot traffic in 24 hours. Park vehicles on the new floor in 48–72 hours. Your Summit County garage is ready for another mountain ski season — backed by lifetime warranty from day one.

Why Cheap Epoxy Fails at Summit County's Elevation

Summit County sits between 9,000 and 11,000 feet above sea level. That altitude creates conditions that destroy poorly installed epoxy garage floors quickly:

  • Extreme freeze-thaw cycling — Summit County garages see more freeze-thaw cycles per winter than almost anywhere in the country. Coatings without proper concrete prep and moisture-blocking primers delaminate from underneath.
  • High moisture vapor transmission — Mountain slabs often sit over ground with significant moisture content. Without a vapor-blocking primer, the coating blisters and peels within 1–2 seasons.
  • Road salt and de-icer exposure — Breckenridge, Frisco, and Silverthorne streets are heavily salted all winter. That salt gets tracked in on ski boots and tires and eats through weak epoxy topcoats fast.
  • Big-box store epoxy kits — Products like Rust-Oleum EpoxyShield are not rated for high-altitude freeze-thaw or mountain salt exposure. We see failed DIY jobs throughout Summit County every spring.
  • UV exposure at elevation — Higher UV at altitude degrades non-UV-stable topcoats. Our polyaspartic topcoats are UV stable and won't yellow or chalk.

Our commercial-grade epoxy and polyaspartic systems have been installed across Summit County and have held up through Colorado's most demanding mountain winters. Every installation comes with a lifetime warranty on delamination and peeling.
