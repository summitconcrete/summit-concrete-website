Why Cheap Epoxy Fails at Breckenridge's Elevation

At 9,600 feet, Breckenridge sees some of the harshest garage-floor conditions in Summit County:

  • Heavy road salt exposure — Breckenridge streets and Highway 9 are salted aggressively all winter. That brine gets tracked in on tires and ski boots and eats through thin topcoats fast.
  • Extreme freeze-thaw cycling — Concrete at this elevation expands and contracts more dramatically than lower-altitude towns. Coatings without proper mechanical prep delaminate from beneath.
  • Intense high-altitude UV — Sun exposure at 9,600 feet is significantly stronger than at sea level. Budget epoxy topcoats yellow and chalk; our polyaspartic topcoats are UV-stable and rated for it.
  • Heavy seasonal use — Many Breckenridge garages double as ski and gear storage, seeing far more foot traffic and moisture than a typical garage.

Every system we install in Breckenridge uses commercial-grade materials chosen specifically for this climate — not an off-the-shelf kit. Our lifetime warranty reflects that.

Caring for Your Floor Through a Breckenridge Winter

A properly installed floor doesn't need much upkeep, but a few habits make a real difference in how it looks after several Breckenridge winters:

  • Rinse off standing salt brine — A quick hose-down or wet-mop after a heavy salt week keeps de-icer residue from sitting on the surface for weeks at a time. It's cosmetic, not structural, but it keeps the floor looking new.
  • Use a squeegee for big snowmelt days — On days when several inches of snow melt off a vehicle at once, pushing the water toward the floor drain or garage door threshold prevents it from pooling in one spot for hours.
  • Don't worry about ice-melt products — Unlike bare concrete, a fully cured polyaspartic topcoat isn't damaged by common calcium chloride or magnesium chloride ice-melt products tracked in on ski boots.
  • Watch for felt or rubber floor mats — Mats that trap moisture underneath for weeks can occasionally cause slight discoloration on any coated floor. Lift and dry them out periodically during heavy ski season.

None of this is required to keep the lifetime warranty valid — it's simply what keeps a Breckenridge garage floor looking as good in year five as it did on installation day.
