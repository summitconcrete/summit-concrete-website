Epoxy Flake Systems

What Is a Full-Broadcast Epoxy Flake Floor?

An epoxy flake floor (sometimes called a chip or fleck system) is a multi-layer coating: a moisture-blocking epoxy base coat, a full broadcast of vinyl flakes thrown to complete coverage — so no bare epoxy shows through — and a clear polyaspartic topcoat that locks everything in. The result looks like granite, hides dirt and dust between cleanings, and adds slip resistance exactly where Colorado garages need it: under wet, salty winter tires.

"Full broadcast" is the phrase that matters. Budget installers scatter a light dusting of flakes over colored epoxy and call it done. We broadcast flake to rejection — the surface literally can't hold any more — which produces a thicker, more uniform, more durable wear layer. It's the difference between a floor that still looks new after ten winters and one that shows tire wear paths in two.

Why Flake Systems Are the Right Call for Mountain Garages

We install flake systems in Steamboat Springs, Summit County, Vail, and Aspen — and the mountain environment is exactly where this system earns its keep:

  • Road salt and mag chloride — The topcoat is chemically resistant to the de-icers CDOT sprays all winter, which etch and dust bare concrete.
  • Freeze-thaw cycling — Diamond-ground prep and a penetrating primer keep the coating bonded through the daily freeze-thaw swings that delaminate cheap coatings.
  • Slip resistance — The flake texture provides grip even when snowmelt pools under your vehicles. We can add an anti-slip aggregate to the topcoat for steeper aprons.
  • Hides real life — The multi-color pattern camouflages mud, gravel, sand, and pine needles between sweepings.
  • UV stability at altitude — Our polyaspartic topcoats won't amber in the intense high-altitude sun that yellows standard epoxy clear coats.

Our Installation Process

Most flake floors are installed in one to two days, and you're parking on the new floor within 48–72 hours:

1

Diamond Grinding & Repair

Commercial diamond grinders open the concrete's pores and remove contaminants. We fill cracks and spalls so the finished floor is smooth and monolithic. Prep is the job — this step is why our floors don't peel.

2

Epoxy Base Coat + Full Flake Broadcast

A moisture-blocking epoxy base goes down, and while it's wet we broadcast your chosen flake blend to full rejection — complete, edge-to-edge coverage with no thin spots.

3

Scrape, Vacuum & Polyaspartic Topcoat

Once cured, we scrape the excess flake flat, vacuum thoroughly, and seal the floor with a UV-stable polyaspartic topcoat that provides the gloss, chemical resistance, and abrasion protection.

4

Walk On in 24 Hours, Park in 48–72

Light foot traffic the next day; vehicles after two to three days. We walk the finished floor with you and register your lifetime warranty before we leave.
