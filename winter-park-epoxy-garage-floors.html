Winter Park & Fraser Valley Epoxy Flooring

Garage Floors Built for the Icebox of the Nation

Fraser has spent decades trading the "Icebox of the Nation" title with International Falls, Minnesota — and anyone who's scraped a windshield in Tabernash in January knows why. The Fraser Valley regularly sees the coldest temperatures of any inhabited valley in Colorado, and that cold is precisely what destroys bargain garage floor coatings: every deep freeze and afternoon thaw flexes the concrete underneath, and a rigid, poorly bonded coating cracks and peels within a season or two.

Our systems are engineered for exactly this environment. We diamond-grind the slab (never acid etch), apply a moisture-blocking primer, and finish with a polyaspartic topcoat that stays flexible through freeze-thaw cycling and shrugs off the road salt and mag chloride tracked in from Highway 40 all winter.

🏔️ Second homes welcome

A large share of our Grand County work is on second homes and short-term rentals. We coordinate directly with property managers and can complete the entire install between guest stays — most projects take just one to two days.

What Affects Your Quote in Winter Park & Grand County

Every garage is different, so we don't quote over the phone. We come to your home — whether that's a slopeside townhome in Winter Park, a ranch property outside Granby, or a lake house in Grand Lake — assess the slab in person, and give you a firm number on the spot. What we're looking at:

  • Concrete condition — Cracks, spalling, and settling are common in Fraser Valley slabs that have seen decades of deep frost; repairs are part of proper prep
  • Moisture — Snow-country slabs often carry moisture vapor that requires a blocking primer to prevent delamination
  • System choice — Full-broadcast flake, solid color, or a grind & seal finish for interior spaces
  • Square footage and layout — Single, 2-car, 3-car, tandem, and shop bays all price differently

Our 2-Day Installation Process

Most epoxy garage floor projects in Winter Park and Grand County are completed in one to two days:

1

Day 1 Morning — Diamond Grinding & Prep

Commercial diamond grinders open the concrete's pores and remove old coatings, oil, and contaminants. Cracks and spalls are repaired. This step is why our floors don't peel — especially critical on slabs that endure Fraser Valley winters.

2

Day 1 Afternoon — Epoxy Base Coat

A moisture-blocking epoxy primer and base coat go down, and we broadcast your chosen flake blend to full coverage while it's wet. The floor cures overnight.

3

Day 2 — Scrape, Clean & Topcoat

We scrape excess flake, vacuum, and apply the polyaspartic topcoat — the UV-stable, salt-resistant wear layer that keeps the floor looking new. Polyaspartic cures properly even in cold weather, which matters up here.

4

48–72 Hours — Vehicle Ready

Light foot traffic in 24 hours, vehicles in 48–72. Your Winter Park garage is back in service before the weekend crowd arrives on the Ski Train.

Why Cheap Epoxy Fails in Grand County

Winter Park sits at 9,052 feet and Fraser at 8,574 — among the highest incorporated towns served by any coating crew in the state. At this elevation:

  • DIY kits acid-etch instead of grinding — etching can't open the concrete enough for a bond that survives 200+ freeze-thaw cycles a year
  • Big-box epoxy is rated for suburban climates — not for slabs that go from sub-zero nights to sun-warmed afternoons all winter long
  • Thin builds wear through fast — ski boots, studded tires, and gravel tracked off Berthoud Pass demand a full-thickness broadcast system
  • Standard epoxy topcoats yellow — high-altitude UV ambers them in a season; our polyaspartic topcoats are permanently UV-stable
