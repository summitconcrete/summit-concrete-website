Our 2-Day Installation Process in Steamboat Springs

Most epoxy garage floor projects in Steamboat Springs are completed in one to two days. Here's exactly what our installation process looks like:

1

Day 1 Morning — Diamond Grinding & Prep

We use commercial diamond grinders to open the concrete's pores and remove any existing coatings, oil stains, or surface contaminants. This is the most critical step — proper prep is what separates a coating that lasts 20 years from one that peels in two.

2

Day 1 Afternoon — Epoxy Base Coat

We apply a moisture-blocking epoxy primer and base coat. While still wet, we broadcast your chosen flake blend across the entire surface for full coverage. The floor cures overnight.

3

Day 2 — Scrape, Clean & Topcoat

We scrape excess flakes, vacuum the surface, and apply a polyaspartic or polyurethane topcoat. This clear protective layer is what gives the floor its gloss, UV stability, and chemical resistance. By end of day, the floor is ready for foot traffic.

4

48–72 Hours — Vehicle Ready

Light foot traffic in 24 hours. You can park your vehicles on the new floor 48–72 hours after installation. Your Steamboat Springs garage is ready to use.

Why Cheap Epoxy Fails in Colorado's Mountain Climate

Steamboat Springs sits at 6,700 feet elevation and sees dramatic temperature swings — from below zero in January to 90°F in summer. This freeze-thaw cycle is the #1 killer of poorly installed epoxy garage floors in Colorado.

Here's why most DIY and budget epoxy jobs fail in Steamboat and across Colorado mountain towns:

  • Improper concrete prep — Acid etching (used in most DIY kits) doesn't open pores deep enough for proper adhesion. Diamond grinding is required for mountain climates.
  • Moisture vapor transmission — Concrete slabs in mountain climates often have high moisture levels. Without a moisture-blocking primer, the coating delaminates from underneath.
  • Wrong product for altitude — Big-box store epoxy kits are not rated for high-altitude temperature extremes or road salt exposure from Colorado winters.
  • Thin topcoat — A single thin coat won't survive ski boots, road salt tracked in from Steamboat's streets, and the weight of full-size mountain vehicles.

Our systems use commercial-grade epoxy and polyaspartic products specifically selected for Colorado's climate — rated for freeze-thaw cycling, road salt, and high UV exposure at elevation.
